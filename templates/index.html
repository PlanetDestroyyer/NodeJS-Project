About Us

Our mission is to save lives by connecting blood donors with those in need, ensuring a steady supply of blood for patients undergoing medical treatment and emergencies.

Where no one suffers due to a lack of blood donations, where every patient has access to safe and timely blood transfusions to support their health and recovery.

Donate Blood

Age: Donors must be at least 18 years old and not more than 65 years old. Some blood banks may accept donations from individuals aged 16 to 17 with parental consent.

Weight: Donors must weigh at least 45 kilograms (approximately 99 pounds).

Health: Donors should be in good health and free from acute illnesses or infections on the day of donation. They should not have any chronic health conditions that could pose a risk to themselves or the recipient of the donated blood.

Hemoglobin Level: Female donors must have a minimum hemoglobin level of 12.5 grams per deciliter (g/dL), while male donors must have a minimum hemoglobin level of 13.0 g/dL.

Travel History: Donors who have traveled to areas with a high prevalence of malaria or other infectious diseases may be deferred from donating blood for a certain period after their return, depending on the specific guidelines of the blood bank.

Medical History: Donors with a history of certain medical conditions or surgeries may be deferred from donating blood, depending on the nature of the condition and its impact on blood safety.

Lifestyle Factors: Individuals who engage in high-risk behaviors such as intravenous drug use, having multiple sexual partners, or engaging in unprotected sex may be deferred from donating blood.

Benefits of Donating Blood

Saving Lives: Blood donation is crucial for providing life-saving transfusions to patients undergoing surgeries, trauma victims, individuals with medical conditions such as cancer or anemia, and those who have experienced severe blood loss due to accidents or childbirth complications.

Supporting Medical Treatments: Blood donations are essential for various medical procedures, including organ transplants, bone marrow transplants, and treatments for certain blood disorders and diseases.

Health Benefits for Donors: Donating blood can have positive health effects for donors, including reducing the risk of certain health conditions such as heart disease, stroke, and cancer. Regular blood donation helps maintain healthy iron levels in the body, which can reduce the risk of iron overload.

Screening for Health Conditions: Blood donation centers conduct screening tests on donated blood to ensure its safety and quality. These tests may detect certain health conditions such as infectious diseases (e.g., HIV, hepatitis) or abnormal blood cell counts, providing donors with valuable health information.

Stimulating Blood Cell Production: After donating blood, the body works to replenish the lost blood volume, which stimulates the production of new blood cells. This process helps maintain the overall health and functionality of the donor's circulatory system.

Sense of Fulfillment: Many donors experience a sense of fulfillment and satisfaction knowing that their donation has made a positive impact on someone else's life. The act of giving blood can foster a sense of community and altruism.

Social Benefits: Blood donation events often bring communities together and raise awareness about the importance of donating blood. Participating in blood drives or donation events can provide opportunities for social interaction and networking.
